Bernard Gardner



Breaking Barriers to Reach for the Stars

"The most successful people see adversity not as a stumbling block, but as a stepping stone to greatness" Shawn Anchor

His life:

  • Born to a working class family. Bernard used to dream about launching rockets into the sky.
  • He wanted to fulfill this dream so much that, as a child, he made home-made gunpowder rockets and a canon that fired lead shots over 400m. Luckily no-one got injured!
  • Gained a place at Oxford University to study Chemistry and Physics despite his poorer, humble background.
  • He started his career working in the national service as a Meterorologist.
  • After completing his national service, he gained a position at the Harwell nuclear reactor and worked with some of the earliest computers. These early computers where so large that they filled rooms.
  • Married and had three children: Steve, Paul and Linda. He then suddenly became a single father with sole custody of the children.
  • He opened one of the first computer shops in the UK (a decade before MS-DOS was released). Computers were a new technology that had not yet reached the wider market. Although despite initially being successful, he lost his business.
  • This was low point in his life that could have broken him.
  • However, he focussed on what his family needed and picked himself up again.
  • He secured a Nuclear Physics job at the European Space Agency. He literally became a rocket scientist!
  • He led a number of projects as the "Space Operations Manager" that involved launching satellites to explore and further our understanding of the solar system and physics.
  • These satellites included: Cos-B , ISEE-2 (International Solar Earth Explorer) and Ulysses.
  • Re-married to Dora and had two children: Andy and Jessica. They were married for many happy years and stayed together for the rest of his life.


Bernard Gardner had a life full of challenges
but he rose above them to achieve his dreams.
 He was an inspiration to his family and those around him.
